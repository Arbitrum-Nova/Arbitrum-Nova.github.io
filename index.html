What is Arbitrum Nova?

Arbitrum Nova is an Arbitrum Nitro AnyTrust chain with Ethereum as its underlying chain, according to the official Arbitrum chain information. It is EVM-compatible and uses ETH for gas.

Arbitrum Nova chain ID, RPC, and explorer

Arbitrum Nova uses chain ID 42170 and ETH as its gas token, matching the ChainList network entry. Nova activity can be checked on the Blockscout transaction explorer. The chain is part of the Arbitrum stack but is not the same network as Arbitrum One, so wallets and bridges must be set to Arbitrum Nova specifically. Developers usually confirm contract deployments against Nova's chain ID, RPC endpoint, and explorer before sending production transactions.

Arbitrum Nova bridge deposits and withdrawals

Arbitrum Nova bridging is mainly used to move ETH and supported tokens between Ethereum and Nova through Arbitrum's bridge interface or compatible third-party routes. Investopedia's cross-chain bridge explainer is a useful neutral reference for how bridge software connects otherwise separate blockchain networks. Users should check that the destination network is Arbitrum Nova, not Arbitrum One, before signing. For larger transfers, a small test transaction is practical because token support, wallet labeling, and exchange deposit support vary by asset and venue.

Arbitrum Nova fees and gas token

Arbitrum Nova fees are paid in ETH, not ARB. The chain was designed for low-cost, high-volume use cases, but the exact fee changes with network demand, calldata or data availability costs, and the transaction type. Users can compare chain-level activity on the DeFiLlama Nova dashboard before deciding whether liquidity and app support fit the intended transfer. If a wallet shows funds but transactions fail, the most common issue is missing ETH on Arbitrum Nova rather than missing ETH on Arbitrum One or Ethereum.

Arbitrum Nova FAQ

Is Arbitrum Nova the same as Arbitrum One?

No. Arbitrum Nova and Arbitrum One are separate chains with separate chain IDs, explorers, liquidity, and bridge routes. The ChainList Arbitrum Nova entry lists Nova with chain ID 42170, while Arbitrum One uses chain ID 42161. A token on one network is not automatically available on the other. When adding a wallet network or sending funds from an exchange, confirm the destination says Arbitrum Nova if that is the chain required by the app.

What token pays gas on Arbitrum Nova?

Arbitrum Nova gas is paid with ETH. ARB is the governance token for the Arbitrum ecosystem, but it is not the gas token for Nova transactions; CoinMarketCap's Arbitrum token profile is a separate market reference for ARB. Users need ETH on Arbitrum Nova specifically to pay for transfers, contract interactions, swaps, NFT mints, and withdrawals. ETH sitting on Ethereum mainnet or Arbitrum One will not pay Nova gas until it is bridged or otherwise moved to Nova.

Where can I see Arbitrum Nova transactions?

Arbitrum Nova transactions can be checked on the Nova explorer, commonly routed through Arbitrum Nova Blockscout. Search by wallet address, transaction hash, token contract, or block number. If a transaction does not appear, confirm that the wallet was connected to Arbitrum Nova rather than Arbitrum One. Also allow time for wallet interfaces and indexers to catch up after a bridge or contract interaction.

Can I bridge USDC to Arbitrum Nova?

USDC availability on Arbitrum Nova depends on the bridge route and issuer or exchange support at the time of transfer. Circle's USDC contract address reference is the kind of source to check before relying on a specific token contract. Some assets may exist as canonical bridge tokens, while others may be routed through third-party bridges or not supported by a given app. For large amounts, use a small test transfer first.

Why is Arbitrum Nova cheaper than Ethereum?

Arbitrum Nova is cheaper because it processes transactions off Ethereum mainnet and uses an AnyTrust data availability model instead of posting all transaction data directly to Ethereum. The official AnyTrust protocol deep dive explains the Data Availability Committee model behind that trade-off. This is why Nova is often positioned for lower-cost app activity rather than every high-value DeFi use case.

Does Arbitrum Nova have an ARB token?

Arbitrum Nova does not use ARB as its gas token. ARB is the governance token for Arbitrum DAO governance, while ETH pays transaction fees on Nova; CoinGecko's ARB market page provides a separate token reference. ARB may be bridged or traded on supported venues, but owning ARB is not required just to send a Nova transaction. The operational token most users need in their Nova wallet is a small amount of ETH for gas.
